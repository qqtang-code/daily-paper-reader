# HKVLM: Faithful Reasoning Grounding by Binding Language Queries to a Frozen Detector

Abstract 

Many visual requests—“the object to open this bottle”, “the person not wearing a helmet”—require reasoning, not just cat-egory matching. Pure open-vocabulary detectors need an ex-plicit phrase; vision-language models (VLMs) can reason yet “see but mis-speak”, attending to the right region but returning the wrong box or label. We argue this is a binding failure: in coordinate-as-text VLMs localization passes through the au-toregressive head, coupling it to language generation; in two-stage pipelines the model’s intent is squeezed through a single class string. We present HKVLM, which removes localization from the language path. A frozen, language-aligned detec-tor emits class-agnostic region proposals; a frozen language model encodes reasoning instructions as referential query embeddings; a lightweight alignment hook binds queries to regions by contrastive retrieval and bipartite assignment in a shared embedding space. A perception-grounded faithful-ness veto forbids naming an object that no region supports. Only the hook is trained, targeting small-data cold-start set-tings where monolithic VLM tuning struggles. We formal-ize a say-vs-see decomposition separating localization er-ror (SeeErr) from binding error (SayErr), and evaluate on RefCOCO/RefCOCO+/RefCOCOg and POPE. With frozen Grounding DINO and Qwen2.5-VL, training only the hook lifts grounding accuracy by 50 –90 × over untrained cross-space matching; the faithfulness veto raises POPE accuracy from near-chance ( 0.50 ) to 0.66 –0.76 and reduces halluci-nation from ∼0.99 to 0.23 –0.43 , with gains from 200 ex-pressions. Increasing proposals from M =50 to M =300 im-proves grounding by 19 –24% without retraining, confirming that residual error is perceptual (SeeErr) rather than binding (SayErr). 

# 1 Introduction 

Practitioners who adapt small open-source VLMs for object detection report a stubborn pattern: out of the box the models localize poorly, and modest fine-tuning—for example low-rank adaptation on a thousand images—barely moves the needle. The models clearly attend to the right pixels yet still return wrong labels or imprecise boxes. We call this the see-but-mis-speak gap. The gap matters most for reasoning grounding : requests phrased as intent or world knowledge rather than a cate-gory name—“something to cut paper with”, “the unoccupied

chair”, “the person not wearing a helmet”—where a pure de-tector has no phrase to match and the system must both reason and localize. This is the regime we target. Closed-set detec-tion, where specialized detectors already excel and language reasoning adds little, is not our battleground. A common explanation blames the visual encoder and pro-poses a stronger one. Encoder upgrades help only marginally in practice, which points elsewhere. In a VLM the encoder output is consumed by the language model exactly as text is: keys and values are read according to a query, and the quality of that query is not guaranteed. When the box itself is produced by the autoregressive decoder—as a sequence of coordinate tokens (Peng et al. 2024; Chen et al. 2023; Zhan et al. 2024a) or quantized coordinate symbols (Bai et al. 2025; Jiang et al. 2026)—the geometry of the object is forced through the same brittle channel that produces words. A single bad query token can override a correct percept. The localization signal and the language signal are entangled. We take the position that faithful grounding requires three 

separable competencies: localization competence (place a tight box on an object), referential faithfulness (name only what is actually there), and binding (connect the user’s intent to the correct region). Coordinate-as-text designs entangle all three inside one autoregressive head. Two-stage reasoning pipelines (Pi et al. 2023; Lai et al. 2024) separate localiza-tion but re-couple through a lossy text interface: the model’s intent is compressed into a class string handed to an exter-nal detector, discarding instance-specific and relational cues. Recent decoupled designs recast box regression as retrieval over proposals (Jiang et al. 2024; Ma et al. 2024), a real step forward, but selection still happens inside the autoregressive language model via index tokens and there is no explicit ob-jective that ties language semantics to region geometry or that penalizes ungrounded assertions. We propose HKVLM, which keeps localization entirely outside the language path. A frozen, language-aligned open-vocabulary detector (Liu et al. 2024; Li et al. 2022) pro-duces class-agnostic, high-recall region proposals together with vision-language-fused region embeddings. The lan-guage model reads the image and the reasoning instruc-tion and emits one or more referential query embeddings 

that carry its world knowledge. A small trained module—the 

alignment hook —projects queries and regions into a shared space and binds them by contrastive retrieval and bipartite

assignment, reusing the set-prediction machinery of DETR (Carion et al. 2020). Categories and answers are produced in language, but every box comes from the detector and every emitted category must be justified by an aligned region whose embedding agrees. This last requirement is a perception-grounded faithfulness constraint, and it is motivated by ev-idence that grounding alone does not automatically reduce hallucination (Geigle, Timofte, and Glavaš 2024). Freezing the heavy components has a practical payoff. Only the hook and a thin projection are trained, so the cold-start regime that defeats parameter-efficient tuning of a monolithic VLM becomes tractable: the localization stack already works, and learning is confined to the binding. Our contributions are: 1. A diagnostic framing that separates localization compe-tence, referential faithfulness, and binding, and a say-vs-see decomposition that measures localization error and naming-given-region error independently (Section 3). 2. HKVLM, a grounding framework in which a frozen de-tector owns localization and an LLM’s referential queries are bound to region embeddings by shared-space align-ment instead of autoregressive coordinate or index tokens (Section 4). 3. A perception-grounded faithfulness objective that sup-presses categories no region supports, and a frozen-stack training recipe targeted at small-data, cold-start adapta-tion (Sections 4). 4. An evaluation protocol—datasets, baselines, metrics, ab-lations, and hypotheses—centered on reasoning and refer-ring grounding (RefCOCO/RefCOCO+/RefCOCOg) and object hallucination (Section 5), together with real image-level results (Section 6) that validate the binding and faithfulness claims at matched perception with a frozen Grounding DINO and a frozen Qwen2.5-VL. We are explicit about scope. Section 6 reports real re-sults on RefCOCO/RefCOCO+/RefCOCOg and POPE that confirm the three hypotheses at matched perception: the trained hook binds 50 –90 × better than untrained cross-space matching, the faithfulness veto converts grounding into hal-lucination resistance, and the hook learns from a few hun-dred expressions. These are mechanism and ablation claims at a fixed, frozen detector, not a state-of-the-art absolute-localization claim: absolute [email protected] is capped by proposal recall under a generic detection prompt. Section 7 adds a controlled embedding-space study that isolates the binding step further (perception assumed perfect). 

# 2 Related Work 

Coordinate-as-text grounding VLMs. Kosmos-2 (Peng et al. 2024) encodes boxes as location tokens and links text spans to regions; Shikra (Chen et al. 2023) renders coordi-nates as natural-language numbers; Ferret (You et al. 2024) mixes discrete points with continuous region features; Grif-fon (Zhan et al. 2024a,b) spells out dense locations as text; and the Qwen-VL family (Bai et al. 2023; Wang et al. 2024; Bai et al. 2025; Qwen Team 2025) decodes absolute coor-dinates. Rex-Omni (Jiang et al. 2026) pushes this paradigm furthest, casting detection as next-token prediction over quan-tized coordinate symbols and reaching detector-level zero-shot accuracy. All of these route geometry through the au-toregressive head. We differ by removing localization from that head entirely; the language model never emits a coordi-nate. 

Reasoning pipelines with a class-string interface. Det-GPT (Pi et al. 2023) has an MLLM name target objects that an open-vocabulary detector then localizes; LISA (Lai et al. 2024) decodes a <SEG> embedding into a mask through SAM (Kirillov et al. 2023). These separate localization but communicate intent through a single string or token, which is lossy for instance-specific and relational queries. We dif-fer by binding a continuous referential query embedding to region embeddings, preserving more of the intent. 

Decoupled retrieval and localized tokenization. Cha-tRex (Jiang et al. 2024) turns box regression into retrieval with a Universal Proposal Network and has the LLM select proposals by index; Groma (Ma et al. 2024) localizes through a region tokenizer referenced by index. Both validate the regression-to-retrieval move that we build on. Referring vari-ants extend this line (IDEA Research 2025), and grounded conversational models attach grounding to chat (Zhang et al. 2024; Rasheed et al. 2024). We differ in where and how selection happens: our binding is an explicit shared-space alignment trained with a matching and faithfulness objec-tive, not an index token emitted by the autoregressive model, and our recipe targets cold-start adaptation rather than large-scale co-training. 

Grounding frozen multimodal models. F-LMM (Wu et al. 2025) keeps the LMM frozen and turns its word–image attention maps into masks with a few trainable layers and a SAM refiner, preserving conversational ability. We share the frozen-stack, light-adapter philosophy. We differ by sourc-ing localization from an external detector bound by explicit alignment, and by adding a faithfulness constraint; F-LMM derives localization from internal attention and targets seg-mentation. A broader trend injects MLLM hidden states as priors to condition detector queries (Sapkota and Karkee 2025); our binding is an explicit retrieval-plus-assignment objective with a perceptual veto rather than a soft prior. 

Open-vocabulary detectors and dense backbones. GLIP (Li et al. 2022), Grounding DINO (Liu et al. 2024), YOLO-World (Cheng et al. 2024), and OWL-ViT (Minderer et al. 2022) detect arbitrary categories from text, and DETR (Car-ion et al. 2020) provides set prediction with bipartite match-ing. Self-supervised backbones (Oquab et al. 2024; Siméoni et al. 2025) and contrastive vision-language pre-training (Radford et al. 2021) yield region features with strong local-ization structure. HKVLM uses these as frozen perception and reuses the matching machinery for binding. 

Hallucination evaluation. POPE (Li et al. 2023) probes object existence with stable yes/no polling, and CHAIR (Rohrbach et al. 2018) measures hallucinated objects in gen-erated text. A cautionary study finds that grounding does not automatically reduce hallucination (Geigle, Timofte, and Glavaš 2024), which motivates our explicit faithfulness ob-jective and the say-vs-see metric rather than an assumption that grounding suffices. 

# 3 Problem Formulation 

Given an image I and a free-form request q (a category name, a referring expression, or an instruction), the system must return a set of category–box pairs {(ci, b i)} that are both accurate (tight, correctly labeled) and faithful (no ci

without supporting evidence in I). We separate three competencies. Localization compe-tence is the ability of a perception module to produce a region set R = {r1, . . . , r M }, with boxes {bj } and embed-dings {ej }, that covers the true objects; we measure it by proposal recall (the fraction of true objects covered by the top M proposals). Binding is the map from the request q

to the correct subset of R. Referential faithfulness is the property that every emitted category is supported by a bound region. 

Say-vs-see decomposition. A grounding error can arise because the right region was never proposed ( see error) or because the right region was available but mis-bound or mis-named ( say error). Let R⋆ ⊆ R be the proposals that overlap a ground-truth object above an IoU threshold. We define

# 4 Method: HKVLM 

HKVLM has four parts: a frozen perception module, a lan-guage module that emits referential queries, the trained align-ment hook, and a faithfulness objective. Figure 1 shows the flow, and Table 1 contrasts HKVLM with prior paradigms. 

4.1 Frozen perception module 

The perception module is a frozen, pre-trained language-aligned open-vocabulary detector. We use Grounding DINO (Liu et al. 2024) (GLIP (Li et al. 2022) is an alternative) and take its class-agnostic, high-recall set of M proposals 

R = {(bj , e j )}Mj=1 , where bj is a box and ej ∈ Rd is the detector’s vision-language-fused region feature for that pro-posal. The choice is deliberate: because these region features already live in a space that has been aligned to text during grounded pre-training, binding them to the language model’s query embeddings is a short bridge rather than a cross-modal leap. A purely self-supervised backbone (DINOv2/DINOv3 (Oquab et al. 2024; Siméoni et al. 2025)), whose features carry no language semantics, is evaluated only as an abla-tion and is expected to make binding harder. The module is frozen, so it carries all localization competence and box quality never depends on language decoding; operating class-agnostically maximizes recall and decouples “what is here” from “which one is meant.” 

4.2 Referential query embeddings 

The language model receives I and q and produces K refer-ential query embeddings {uk}Kk=1 , one per entity the request implies. We read these from dedicated query slots appended to the prompt, so the model’s reasoning and world knowl-edge shape uk while the language model itself can remain frozen or be adapted with low-rank updates. Crucially uk is a continuous vector, not a coordinate or an index token; it preserves more of the intent than a class string.

# 5 Experimental Design and Evaluation Protocol 

We specify the study that tests HKVLM’s claims. The pro-tocol below defines tasks, baselines, metrics, ablations, and hypotheses; Section 6 reports the subset we have run on real images at matched perception. 

Tasks and datasets. The primary task is reasoning and referring grounding: return the box for a free-form, often knowledge-dependent request. We use Ref-COCO/RefCOCO+/RefCOCOg (Yu et al. 2016) over COCO images (Lin et al. 2014), with RefCOCOg emphasized for its longer, more compositional expressions, and a reasoning-grounding setting with implicit queries (paraphrased into intent/world-knowledge form). Faithfulness is measured on POPE (Li et al. 2023) (and CHAIR (Rohrbach et al. 2018) for generated descriptions). Open-vocabulary generalization to unseen categories (e.g., long-tail classes (Gupta, Dollár, and Girshick 2019)) is a secondary, zero-shot check rather than the main result. 

Baselines. The closest competitor is the class-string rea-soning pipeline DetGPT (Pi et al. 2023) (LLM names a phrase, an open-vocabulary detector localizes); coordinate-as-text VLMs on referring (Chen et al. 2023; Bai et al. 2025; Zhan et al. 2024a); the decoupled-retrieval model ChatRex (Jiang et al. 2024); and frozen-LMM grounding (Wu et al. 2025). For fairness, HKVLM, DetGPT, and ChatRex are paired with the same frozen Grounding DINO so differences reflect the binding interface, not the detector. 

Metrics. Grounding accuracy (the top-ranked region is counted correct when its overlap with the true referent reaches IoU 0.5) on RefCOCO/+/g and the reasoning split; POPE accuracy/F1 under random, popular, and adversarial sampling; CHAIR s/CHAIR i; our SeeErr and SayErr ; pro-posal recall; and efficiency (trainable parameters, latency). 

Ablations. (i) Replace the alignment hook with an AR-index variant (to isolate shared-space alignment vs. index retrieval) and with a class-string variant (to isolate the con-tinuous query, i.e., a DetGPT-style interface). (ii) Remove 

Lfaith and the veto (to measure faithfulness on POPE-adversarial and SayErr). (iii) Swap the frozen perception from the language-aligned detector (Liu et al. 2024) to a self-supervised backbone (Siméoni et al. 2025) (to confirm the language-alignment design choice). (iv) Freeze the lan-guage model vs. LoRA. (v) Vary M (recall vs. precision). 

Hypotheses. H1: on reasoning/referring grounding, HKVLM attains higher grounding accuracy and lower 

SayErr than the class-string pipeline and coordinate-as-text VLMs at matched perception. H2: the faithfulness veto im-proves POPE (especially adversarial) with negligible recall loss. H3: training only the hook reaches strong grounding from a few hundred to a few thousand expressions, beating low-rank tuning of a monolithic VLM at equal data. 

Data-efficiency study. Train at increasing numbers of re-ferring expressions (e.g., 0.5k, 1k, 5k, full) and compare HKVLM (hook only) against low-rank tuning of a mono-lithic VLM at equal budget, reporting grounding accuracy versus data size. # 6 Results 

We instantiate HKVLM end-to-end on real images. Percep-tion is a frozen Grounding DINO (Liu et al. 2024) that emits class-agnostic top-M proposals with 256 -dimensional vision-language-fused region features; queries come from a 

frozen Qwen2.5-VL-7B (Bai et al. 2025) that reads each image and referring expression and returns one 3584 -dimensional referential query embedding. Only the align-ment hook (two projections, the box residual, and the veri-fier) is trained, on 24 k RefCOCOg (Yu et al. 2016) training expressions; the veto thresholds (γv , γ s) are fixed on held-out data. We evaluate on RefCOCO, RefCOCO+, and Ref-COCOg (Yu et al. 2016) (referring grounding) and on POPE (Li et al. 2023) random/popular/adversarial (object halluci-nation). Two references share the identical frozen perception, isolating the binding interface: naive cosine , which matches queries to regions through untrained projections, and no-veto , the full hook with the faithfulness objective and veto removed.

# 7 Proof-of-Concept Validation 

To complement the real-image results of Section 6, we fur-ther isolate the part HKVLM actually learns—the binding step—in a controlled simulation that removes pixel percep-tion from the picture. Each scene provides M frozen region embeddings; a referent’s language-side query embedding and its detector-side region embedding are noisy images of the same instance latent under two different linear maps, repro-ducing the modality gap the alignment hook must bridge. Because the correct region is always present for positive queries, SeeErr = 0 by construction and every error is a binding ( SayErr ) error—exactly the quantity we target. This is a mechanism check, not a COCO/LVIS result. We compare the trained hook against two references imple-mentable in this setting: naive cosine , which matches queries to regions with untrained projections (a shared space with-out learning), and class-string , which collapses the query to its nearest class and picks the first region of that class (a lossy text interface that cannot disambiguate same-class instances). We report top-1 binding accuracy, existence F1 (a query emits only when it passes the faithfulness veto and binds correctly), and a POPE-style yes/no existence accuracy. # 8 Discussion, Limitations, and Broader Impact 

The clearest limitation is visible directly in our numbers: grounding accuracy is upper-bounded by frozen-proposal re-call, so the absolute accuracy of ∼0.26 (Table 2, M =300 )reflects residual SeeErr under a generic detection prompt— HKVLM cannot bind to an object the detector never pro-posed. Increasing M from 50 to 300 improves grounding by 

19 –24% without any hook retraining, confirming SeeErr as the dominant remaining error; recall-oriented, expression-conditioned proposing is the natural next step. We also ob-serve that a larger proposal pool raises the false-positive rate of the veto (hallucination increases from ≤0.14 at M =50 

to 0.23 –0.43 at M =300 at the same threshold), indicating that the faithfulness threshold γv should be jointly optimized with M in future work. Our evidence is further confined to two ablation references sharing the frozen perception; head-to-head comparison with external systems (DetGPT, ChatRex, coordinate-as-text VLMs) and low-rank VLM tun-ing at equal data budget are reserved for future work. The design also assumes region embeddings carry enough se-mantic signal for shared-space binding; for very fine-grained or relational requests a single query embedding may be insuf-ficient, suggesting multi-query or compositional extensions. Masks are a natural extension by attaching a promptable seg-menter (Kirillov et al. 2023) to bound boxes. Finally, faithful open-vocabulary detection can be misused for surveillance; the same veto that suppresses hallucination should be paired with deployment controls and dataset governance. # 9 Conclusion 

We reframed the see-but-mis-speak problem of ground-ing VLMs as a binding failure and proposed HKVLM, which gives localization to a frozen detector and re-binds it to language by shared-space alignment with a perception-grounded faithfulness veto, rather than by autoregressive coordinate or index tokens. On Ref-COCO/RefCOCO+/RefCOCOg and POPE, at matched frozen perception, training only the hook lifts grounding ac-curacy by 50 –90 × over untrained cross-space matching, the faithfulness veto raises POPE from near-chance to 0.66 –0.76 

while substantially reducing object hallucination, and the hook reaches useful accuracy from 200 training expressions. Increasing the proposal budget from M =50 to M =300 im-proves grounding by 19 –24% without hook retraining, pin-pointing residual SeeErr as the dominant remaining error and validating the say-vs-see decomposition as a diagnostic tool.

# 3 Say-vs-See: Full Definitions 

For an image with ground-truth objects G and proposals R,let R⋆ = {r ∈ R : ∃g ∈ G , IoU( r, g ) ≥ τ }. SeeErr is the fraction of ground-truth objects with no overlapping pro-posal; it depends only on perception and the chosen M and 

τ . SayErr is the conditional probability of an incorrect label or mis-binding when the correct region is present in R⋆; it isolates the language-binding stage. Reporting both attributes each grounding failure to perception or to binding, and lets us verify that HKVLM lowers SayErr without inflating SeeErr (perception is frozen). 

# 4 Extended Evaluation Protocol 

Detector/backbone grid. Cross frozen detectors (Liu et al. 2024b; Cheng et al. 2024) with frozen backbones (Oquab et al. 2024; Siméoni et al. 2025) to attribute gains to per-ception versus binding; report mAP, proposal recall, SeeErr, SayErr per cell. 

Binding ablations. (a) alignment hook (ours); (b) AR-index selection in the style of decoupled retrieval (Jiang et al. 2024; Ma et al. 2024); (c) class-string handoff (Pi et al. 2023). Same perception and data for all three to isolate the binding mechanism. 

Faithfulness ablations. Toggle Lfaith and the veto; sweep 

(γv , γ s) to plot a faithfulness-recall curve on POPE (Li et al. 2023) and CHAIR (Rohrbach et al. 2018). 

Cold-start curve. Train the hook at {0.5k, 1k, 5k} images and compare to low-rank tuning of a monolithic VLM (Wang et al. 2024; Liu et al. 2024a) at equal data, plotting mAP and RefCOCO accuracy versus data size.
